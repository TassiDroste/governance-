About the Governance Compass

Governance is multidimensional. No single index tells the full story — corruption scores miss the question of civic freedoms; press freedom rankings say nothing about rule of law; effectiveness measures leave out accountability. The Governance Compass is an attempt to bring these dimensions together into a single, comparable score.

It combines up to eight internationally recognised indices — from Transparency International, the World Bank, Freedom House, the World Justice Project, Reporters Without Borders, the Mo Ibrahim Foundation, and the Chandler Institute of Governance, and the Bertelsmann Stiftung — normalised to a common 0–100 scale and averaged equally. Only countries with data from at least four indices are included, to avoid spurious precision.

The result is not a new index — it is an editorial synthesis, a practical tool for comparison. It should be read alongside the individual indices, not instead of them. Click any country to see which indices contributed to its score, how it performs on each dimension, and how its governance has changed over the past five years.

Corruption Perceptions Index (CPI)
Transparency International · Since 1995
Aggregates expert assessments and business surveys from 13 independent institutions to measure perceived levels of public sector corruption. A composite index drawing on data from surveys of business executives, country analysts, and public officials.

World Bank Governance Indicators (WGI)
World Bank · Since 1996
Six dimensions of governance: Voice and Accountability, Political Stability, Government Effectiveness, Regulatory Quality, Rule of Law, and Control of Corruption. The map shows the Control of Corruption dimension. Compiled from over 30 data sources covering 215 economies.

Mo Ibrahim Index of African Governance (IIAG)
Mo Ibrahim Foundation · Since 2007
Measures governance across four categories: Security and Rule of Law, Participation, Rights and Inclusion, Foundations for Economic Opportunity, and Human Development. Africa only. Based on 81 indicators compiled from 39 independent African and global data sources.
0–10054 African countriesAnnual
Freedom in the World Index
Freedom House · Since 1972
Assesses political rights and civil liberties through a methodology based on the Universal Declaration of Human Rights. Analysts evaluate 25 indicators grouped under two broad categories: Political Rights (electoral processes, political pluralism, government functioning) and Civil Liberties (freedoms of expression, assembly, rule of law, personal autonomy).
0–100195 countriesAnnual
Press Freedom Index
Reporters Without Borders · Since 2002
Evaluates the environment for journalism across five contexts: political, legal, economic, sociocultural, and security. Combines a questionnaire completed by media specialists, lawyers, and sociologists with quantitative data on abuses against journalists. Score shown here is inverted (100 = most free).
0–100180 countriesAnnual
WJP Rule of Law Index
World Justice Project · Since 2008
Measures rule of law across eight factors: Constraints on Government Powers, Absence of Corruption, Open Government, Fundamental Rights, Order and Security, Regulatory Enforcement, Civil Justice, and Criminal Justice. Based on surveys of over 149,000 households and 3,500 legal experts in 142 countries.
0–100142 countriesAnnual
Bertelsmann Transformation Index (BTI)
Bertelsmann Stiftung · Since 2003 · Biennial
Measures the quality of governance in developing and transition countries across three dimensions: political transformation (democracy, rule of law, stability), economic transformation (market economy, performance), and governance quality (Management Index). Based on assessments by nearly 300 country experts. Covers 137 countries; excludes OECD members consolidated before 1989.
0–100137 countriesBiennial

Measures government capabilities and effectiveness across seven pillars: Leadership and Foresight, Robust Laws and Policies, Strong Institutions, Financial Stewardship, Attractive Marketplace, Global Influence and Reputation, and Helping People Rise. Built by government practitioners, drawing on 35 indicators from international data sources.

Governance Compass — methodology

This score is an editorial synthesis combining up to eight major governance indices into a single 0–100 scale. It is intended as a practical overview tool, not a standalone index.

Normalisation: Each index is rescaled to 0–100 before combination. The World Bank WGI (originally −2.5 to +2.5) is rescaled linearly. All others are already on a 0–100 scale. The Press Freedom Index (RSF) is already inverted so that higher = more free.

Weighting: Equal weights are applied — each available index contributes 1/n to the composite, where n is the number of indices with data for that country. Note: BTI is biennial (2020, 2022, 2024 only) and covers developing/transition countries; Western democracies show as no-data for BTI in the selected year.

Coverage threshold: Only countries with data for at least 4 of the 8 indices are included. Countries with fewer data points are shown in grey.

Caveat: The Mo Ibrahim Index covers Africa only, meaning African countries have up to one additional data point. Country scores are not strictly comparable where the set of contributing indices differs. Always check which indices are included in a country's score by clicking the country.
